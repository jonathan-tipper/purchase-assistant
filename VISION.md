# Vision: Purchase Assistant

## Vision

Purchase Assistant helps people buy with clarity by combining transparent ownership math, lightweight reflection, and AI-guided questioning in one focused decision workspace.

The product should become a trusted pre-purchase and post-purchase companion: simple enough to use before an everyday buy, but structured enough to improve major purchase decisions over time.

## Product Belief

The best purchase is not always the cheapest item or the premium item. It is the item whose cost, usage, lifespan, depreciation, and satisfaction fit the user's real life.

Purchase Assistant should help users slow down just enough to see those tradeoffs before money is spent.

## Differentiators

- Transparent calculations: Users can see and edit the assumptions behind every recommendation.
- Ownership lens: The app evaluates value over time rather than only upfront price.
- Reflection loop: The journal connects future decisions to actual satisfaction from past purchases.
- AI as a thinking partner: AI helps estimate, compare, and reflect, but the user remains in control of the decision.
- Portable data: Import and export features support user ownership of purchase data.

## Experience Principles

- Start with the calculator, not account creation.
- Prefer clear metrics over persuasion.
- Keep AI outputs concise, editable, and grounded in visible assumptions.
- Make comparison easy when a user is choosing between alternatives.
- Treat regret and satisfaction as useful learning signals, not moral judgments.
- Preserve trust by keeping data behavior, AI limits, and account requirements explicit.

## Future Opportunities to Validate

- Unified local-first and account-synced item storage.
- Richer purchase categories, notes, images, and budgets.
- Saved advisor conversations and decision histories.
- Better receipt or transaction import flows.
- Price tracking, resale value references, or warranty reminders.
- Product-specific assumption presets for common categories.
- Longitudinal insights that connect predicted value to actual satisfaction.

These are opportunities, not committed roadmap priorities.

## What the Product Should Not Become

- A shopping feed optimized for conversion.
- A black-box AI recommender that hides assumptions.
- A personal finance product that gives regulated financial advice.
- A data-harvesting tool that makes export or deletion hard.
- A bloated inventory system for every owned item.
